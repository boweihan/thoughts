On Software Engineering (without elaboration)

----------------------------------------------------------------

> Instead of wondering if you could, wonder if you should.

> Everything has a trade-off; no decision is perfect.

> The most valuable technical skill is "connecting the dots".

> Strong ownership is the solution to most problems.

> Make employee sustainability a performance metric.

> Don't forget to decouple your architecture after you decouple your organization.

> Architecture is a shared understanding, not a fancy diagram.

> Most decisions are worth writing down.

> Most good decisions are incremental.

> Asynchronous standups suck.
